Good Good Wonderful are building ways to find and benefit from the awesome skills in our community and then pay it forward by thanking others.

- If you want to borrow a mower from a neighbor and then be sure to return the favor, we can help you keep track.

- If you want to find someone to fix that broken step, but can’t afford to pay, here’s how to get that done then pay it forward by helping someone else.

- Or, if you want to build your dream startup, but only have skills of a particular type and no money to pay for the skills you’re missing, here’s a way you can trade on the skills you have to get the skills you need.

And here’s the best part...

Whether it's helping others or receiving good things in return being part of these communities doesn’t cost you anything other than your own promise to pay it forward.

About Us

Our vision

We’re building a deeply transparent and open organisation commited to doing this the right way - not by charity but by a commitment to open source, honoring community and using our passion for good design and good tools to make a lasting and permanent difference in this world.

Why?

Too many people in our society are undervalued, underappreciated and under paid. Every member of our society has gifts to offer it is time we learned how to appreciate them.

Too much of society is organised by the rules of a debt based banking system taking us on a dangerous and unstable journey into unsustainable growth and destruction.

Good Good Wonderful was started by people who want to do something about these problems.

We believe there are better ways to encourage each of us to share the gifts and skills we have to offer with the rest of the world. We believe there are better and more powerful ways to say thanks and pay it forward, that can take us down that path.

We want to demonstrate that there is a worthwhile, powerful, and sustainable way for us to organise ourselves without having to resort to the money system. And if along the way we demonstrate a new form of community that can be copied by others who want to be resilient to the next economic crash or to the next natural disaster, all the better.
